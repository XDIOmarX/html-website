The Matrix (1999)

Directors: Lilly & Lana Wachowski  |  Runtime: 2h 16m  |  Rating: 8.7 / 10

Synopsis

Thomas Anderson, a software engineer by day and hacker named Neo by night, is contacted by Morpheus, who reveals the world he inhabits is a simulation — the Matrix — designed by machines to keep humanity docile.

After taking the red pill and waking in the real world, Neo trains to become "The One," a prophesied liberator, and must battle Agent Smith to bring down the Matrix from within.

Key facts

Directors Lilly & Lana Wachowski
Year 1999
Technique invented Bullet-time photography
Oscar wins 4 (including editing & visual effects)
Box office $467 million worldwide

Why it matters

The Wachowskis invented "bullet-time" by placing hundreds of still cameras around the subject to freeze a moment in time. The technique was immediately copied worldwide. The film also popularised simulation theory and cyber-philosophy for mass audiences.

"Unfortunately, no one can be told what the Matrix is. You have to see it for yourself."

— Morpheus (Laurence Fishburne)
