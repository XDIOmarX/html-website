Interstellar (2014)

Director: Christopher Nolan  |  Runtime: 2h 49m  |  Rating: 8.7 / 10

Synopsis

In a near-future where blight is destroying Earth's crops, former NASA pilot Cooper is recruited for a secret mission: travel through a wormhole near Saturn to find a habitable planet in another galaxy.

The mission confronts the crew with real effects of general relativity — time passes far slower near massive objects, meaning Cooper could return to find his daughter aged far beyond him.

Key facts

Director Christopher Nolan
Year 2014
Science advisor Kip Thorne (Nobel laureate in Physics)
Composer Hans Zimmer
Oscar win Best Visual Effects

Why it matters

Physicist Kip Thorne worked directly with the visual effects team to render "Gargantua," the film's black hole, accurately. The simulation was so precise it contributed to a published scientific paper on gravitational lensing.

"We used to look up at the sky and wonder at our place in the stars. Now we just look down and worry about our place in the dirt."

— Cooper (Matthew McConaughey)
