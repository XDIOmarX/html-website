Arrival (2016)

Director: Denis Villeneuve  |  Runtime: 1h 56m  |  Rating: 7.9 / 10

Synopsis

When twelve mysterious spacecraft appear around the world, linguist Louise Banks is recruited by the military to attempt communication with the alien "Heptapods" aboard one of the vessels hovering over Montana.

As Louise learns the aliens' circular written language, she begins to experience non-linear visions. The film slowly reveals that language doesn't just describe time — it can change how we experience it.

Key facts

Director Denis Villeneuve
Year 2016
Based on "Story of Your Life" by Ted Chiang
Linguist consultant Jessica Coon (McGill University)
Composer Jóhann Jóhannsson
Oscar win Best Sound Editing

Why it matters

Arrival is the rare sci-fi film where the central "weapon" is linguistics. It takes the idea that language shapes thought and builds a cosmological thriller around it. The film doesn't just ask "are we alone?" — it asks what we would sacrifice to truly understand the answer.

"If you could see your whole life from start to finish, would you change things?"

— Louise Banks (Amy Adams)
