2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)

Director: Stanley Kubrick  |  Runtime: 2h 29m  |  Rating: 8.3 / 10

Synopsis

The story unfolds across four movements. It begins in prehistoric Africa, where early hominids encounter a mysterious black monolith that triggers a leap in intelligence. Millions of years later, Dr. Heywood Floyd travels to the Moon to investigate a similar monolith buried beneath the surface.

The central chapter follows astronauts Dave Bowman and Frank Poole aboard the spacecraft Discovery One, bound for Jupiter. Their mission is secretly overseen by the ship's sentient computer, HAL 9000, whose breakdown leads to one of cinema's most chilling confrontations between human and machine.

The final act is a psychedelic journey through space and time that ends with Bowman transformed into the mysterious Star Child.

Key facts

Director Stanley Kubrick
Year 1968
Based on "The Sentinel" by Arthur C. Clarke
Budget $10.5 million
Oscar win Best Visual Effects

Why it matters

Before CGI existed, Kubrick's team built rotating sets to create weightlessness. The match cut from bone to spacecraft remains the most celebrated single edit in movie history. HAL 9000 became the archetypal fictional AI — calm, logical, and ultimately dangerous.
